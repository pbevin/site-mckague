Notes for James HAMILTON
James was born in Ireland. "A rich and rather pompous widower." He lived in Belleville ON.26

His will, dated Mar. 14, 1898, leaves his estate (value $35,710.00) to his wife Nancy Jane. He left an allowance of $300 yearly to his son John D. Hamilton while unmarried. He also left his son "my gold watch and chain, to be given to him by my said executors at such time only as they shall think his mode of life to be such as to entitle him to such." One paragraph gives further instructions if his son married "to the satisfaction and with the consent" of the executors. Executors were James's brother and brother-in-law Alexander Moore Hamilton and Alexander Edward Ward Peterson.26

Obituary from Belleville Daily Intelligencer, May 1, 1900
"MR. JAMES HAMILTON DEAD
"A well-known resident of the city has gone to his reward
"Mr. James Hamilton residing on Alexander Street died shortly after noon today. The deceased was one of Belleville's best known and much respected citizens. He was born in Ireland upwards of 75 years ago. When a lad he came to this country with his parents and in early life lived in the township of Thurlow. In after years Mr. Hamilton for some time conducted a general store at Warkworth. In the year 1877 he removed to Belleville where he has since resided. Deceased was twice married, being survived by his second wife who was a Miss Peterson of Colborne. One son, John, who is in Cleveland, is the only child. Mr. Hamilton was a member of Bridge Street Church. In politics he was a staunch Conservative."107
