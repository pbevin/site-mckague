Notes for William Edward McCREA
Edward's family was Loyalist of Scottish ancestry from New York State. They settled at Merrickville in Grenville County ON. This is where Edward met his wife Mary Knapp.18

"EDWARD McCREA, the second child of Samuel McCrea was born at or near Stillwater, New York, on June 19, 1779. When he was fourteen years of age, he left Stillwater with his parents and family for their new home in Canada. ...
"He became an extensive land-owner in the area about Merrickville, Ontario, and, as his great grandson, WALTER McRAYE, wrote in his reminiscences 'Town Hall Tonight', - 'he was a man of character and worth'. He had a large stone house built on a part of the homestead of his father which still stands today, although it is now in a bad state of repair."32

Edward served in the Patriots' Army during the1837 Upper Canada Rebellion.18

"A gentleman possessing an elegant residence. He is an active Justice of the Peace, and one of the most influential and prosperous farmers of Montague."114
