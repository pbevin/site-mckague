Notes for Guillaume RICHARD dit LA FLEUR
Guillaume joined the Régiment Carignan-Callières on its formation in 1664. On May 31, 1665, he left with the regiment for New France in the company of Captain Gauthier de la Varennes. They were sent in an attempt to save the distressed New France from repeated attacks by the British and their Iroquois allies. The population had dwindled to about 2,500, of whom about 500 were in Québec. They arrived in Québec Aug 19, 1665 after a long and tempestuous voyage. Guillaume served largely in the Montréal area. When the regiment returned to France in 1667, he chose to remain behind.122

He was shortly thereafter appointed sergeant of the Montreal militia, and accompanied Count Frontenac up the St. Lawrence River in July 1673 to establish an outpost at Cataraqui, later known as Fort Frontenac. When Frontenac and his party left about two weeks later, Guillaume, with 15 men, was left in charge of the outpost until La Salle returned in 1675 to take command.111 He then returned to Montréal and was married to Agnes Tessier; his residence at marriage was Montréal Parish. They returned to Fort Frontenac.117 He was still there, according to a census of the Fort, in 1677, and apparently remained until 1679.111

He was then appointed sergeant of the garrison at Montréal. By 1689 he was Lieutenant of the Vanguard Company at Montréal. He later served as captain of militia in the parish of Pointe-aux-Trembles de Montréal, where he had been living since 1679.111 [But note that on the 1681 census he is listed as living in Montréal Parish with his wife Agnes and three children: Agnes, Pierre and Guillaume. At his son Jean-Baptiste's baptism on 1682, their residence is given as Pointe-aux-Trembles Parish.]113

Guillaume and a party of twenty-five men were slaughtered by a band of Iroquois warriors on July 2, 1690 near Bout-de-l'Ile de Montréal. Their bodies were discovered six days later, and they were buried at the site. They were later re-interred at Pointe-aux-Trembles.111

Guillaume had 12 children.111

Guillaume was unable to sign at his marriage.113

The family is shown in the 1681 census of Montréal as follows:
Guillaume RICHARD, 40, "marechal" [marshal], Agnes TESSIER, 23, Agnes, 4, Pierre, 3, Guillaume, 2.113
